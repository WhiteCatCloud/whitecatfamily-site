The Problem

  • Kids bypass app-based controls with VPNs and DNS changes
  • Most parental apps monetize browsing data
  • Controls break when devices update or leave Wi-Fi
  • Parents are forced into child surveillance instead of boundaries

The WhiteCat Approach

  • Network-level enforcement – rules apply before traffic reaches devices
  • Local processing – decisions are made on the router, not in the cloud
  • No child accounts – nothing to log into, nothing to uninstall
  • Works for all devices – phones, tablets, TVs, consoles, IoT

How It Works (Technical Overview)

  • DNS requests are inspected and filtered locally on the router
  • Time-based rules apply per device or user group
  • Blocked services resolve to safe responses or are denied entirely
  • Unauthorized VPN and DNS-over-HTTPS attempts are blocked
  • Optional cloud access is used only for rule sync and updates

Browsing history stays local unless you explicitly enable reports.

Privacy by Design

  • No third-party trackers
  • No child behavior profiling

WhiteCat is built to enforce limits, not spy.

FAQ

Can kids bypass this with VPNs?

No. Unauthorized VPN and DNS traffic is blocked at the router level.

Is browsing data uploaded?

No. Browsing stays local unless reports are explicitly enabled.

Does this work when kids leave home?

Network rules apply at home. Remote management controls rules, not mobile data filtering.

Do children need an app?

No. Nothing to install, remove, or log into.
