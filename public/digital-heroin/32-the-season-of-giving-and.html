🎁 The Season of Giving (and Scamming)

Christmas is supposed to be the happiest part of the year. But for scammers, this is harvest season. More shopping, more parcels, more tired and distracted people. Perfect conditions to rob families quietly.

1️⃣ The Fake Delivery Trap — You're waiting for gifts. Your phone buzzes: "Your package couldn't be delivered. Click here to reschedule." Looks like UPS, FedEx, DHL. Logo looks right. Just a small redelivery fee. Stop ‼️ One tap → fake tracking page → asking for card number.
Family rule: Never click tracking links from SMS or email. Go directly to the official app or website.

2️⃣ "Too Good To Be True" Christmas Deals — The console that's sold out everywhere — somehow in stock on a shady website. The countdown timer screams "Last chance!" Stop ‼️
Family rule: If a site is unknown and the price is crazy low, it's not a bargain — it's bait.

3️⃣ "Pay Me in Gift Cards" — Classic, but still working every day. Scammer asks for gift cards and the codes. Stop ‼️ Once you send the code, money is gone. No chargeback. No refund. No trace.
Family rule: No serious company, no bank, no government office ever demands payment in gift cards.

4️⃣ Fake Christmas Charities — Real charities ask for support, but scammers copy their names and logos. Stop ‼️
Family rule: If you want to donate, go directly to the charity's official website — never through a random email or social post.
