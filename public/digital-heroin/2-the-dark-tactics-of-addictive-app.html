💊 The Dark Tactics of Addictive App Design

Social media apps and games aren't just fun — they're engineered to hijack your brain. Features like infinite scroll, autoplay videos, and push notifications are deliberate traps designed to exploit your psychological triggers.

Ever notice how Instagram Reels keep playing or how a single notification pulls you right back in? That's no accident. It's called the "lure of the next reward," driven by FOMO (fear of missing out) and dopamine spikes.

Ex-Google designer Tristan Harris calls it "time on device optimization" — maximizing your screen time at the cost of your well-being.

Leaked internal Meta documents, revealed by whistleblower Frances Haugen in 2021, show their own research confirmed their algorithms amplify addictive behaviors — yet engagement always came first.

It's a digital casino — and you're the player. Who's cashing in on your attention?
