Session 3 · Part 1

Screen Time and the Sleep Crisis

Picture this: a kid lying in bed, phone hidden under the blanket. Eyes wide open, scrolling, tapping, laughing quietly at a TikTok. Outside the door, parents think they're asleep. But inside the room, the brain is getting tricked — blue light screaming daytime, dopamine firing like it's Vegas. And sleep? It doesn't stand a chance.

Kids today sleep nearly two hours less than their parents did at the same age. Chronic sleep loss is tied to anxiety, depression, obesity, and weakened immune systems. Teachers report kids yawning in class, losing focus — not because they're lazy, but because their brains never shut off.

We thought screens were stealing attention. Turns out, they're stealing childhood nights too.

Session 2 · Part 6

The Dark Side of Streaming

Your TV isn't passive anymore. It's watching you too. Every pause, rewind, or late-night binge goes into a log. That data gets stitched into your profile: Are you a night owl? Do you linger on kids' shows after the kids leave the room? Advertisers love this.

For kids, it's worse. Autoplay turns cartoons into babysitters. Bright thumbnails make sure they never run out of "just one more." For seniors, the danger shifts — rogue "TV apps" and fake pop-ups trick them into handing over credit card numbers.

Streaming didn't just kill cable. It turned the family sofa into a data pipeline.

Session 4 · Part 3

Malware Disguised as Fun Apps

A new game. A flashlight app. A photo editor with cute stickers. Behind the friendly icon hides something darker — malware, quietly siphoning data, contacts, photos, even location. Kids love to explore. Parents love convenience. Hackers love both.

In 2024, researchers found hundreds of Android apps that secretly installed tracking code targeting children. Some collected voice samples through the microphone. Others linked data to third-party advertising networks in countries with zero privacy laws.

That "game" your 9-year-old downloaded may have sent your home's Wi-Fi password to a server overseas.

The Tech Giants' Playbook

Predatory Design — and Your Kids Are the Target

Every "free" app has a hidden manual — not for you, but for the designers. Inside this playbook: psychological warfare exploiting FOMO and reward loops, personalized bait tracking every click, push notifications and streaks to keep you checking, and infinite content streams with no stopping point.

The same techniques are used in casinos and gambling apps — but in your pocket, 24/7. This isn't bad design. It's predatory design. And your kids are the easiest targets.

The question isn't whether your child will be affected — it's how much damage is already done.
